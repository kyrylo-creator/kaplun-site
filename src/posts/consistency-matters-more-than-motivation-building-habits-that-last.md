Many people decide to change their lives at the beginning of a new year, on a Monday, or after an important life event. They feel inspired to exercise, eat healthier, become more productive, or finally take care of their mental well-being.

Inspiration is a wonderful place to start. But by itself, it rarely leads to lasting change.

When I used to run, we would often smile at those who sprinted away from the starting line. They looked impressive for the first hundred metres, but soon they ran out of energy. Those who chose a steady, comfortable pace were usually the ones who reached the finish line.
Life works in much the same way.

### **Why Motivation Isn't Enough?**

Motivation naturally rises and falls. It depends on your mood, stress levels, physical health, and everyday circumstances.
Consistency, on the other hand, creates stability. Small actions repeated regularly become healthy habits, and healthy habits gradually transform your life.
The best routine is not the most demanding one—it is the one you can realistically maintain over time.

### **Building Sustainable Habits**

Lasting change doesn't require dramatic action. Instead, it helps to:
•	set realistic goals;
•	take small, manageable steps;
•	balance work, rest, and personal time;
•	accept setbacks without self-criticism;
•	focus on progress rather than perfection.
This steady approach supports both physical and mental well-being.
When Sticking to a Routine Feels Difficult

### **Sometimes the problem isn't a lack of willpower.**

Anxiety, chronic stress, emotional burnout, low mood, or difficult life experiences can drain your energy and make even simple daily tasks feel overwhelming.
Psychological counselling can help you understand what's getting in the way, strengthen your inner resources, and develop routines that truly fit your life rather than someone else's expectations.
